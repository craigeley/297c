Assignments and Grading

  • Weekly Responses (10%): Students will be asked to provide short but insightful weekly responses to the readings. These are due in advance There will be 11 of these assigned throughout the semester, and students will be asked to complete 10 of them. The questions will be posted each week on Thursday and due before class on the following Tuesday. See more details here.
  • Audio Assignment 1 (10%): This assignment will cover the basics of audio editing. See more details here.
  • Audio Assignment 2 (10%): This assignment will consist of a short sound piece recorded and edited by the student. See more details here.
  • Writing Assignment 1 (10%): This will be a take-home essay exam. The questions, details, and deadlines will be provided here.
  • Writing Assignment 2 (10%): This will be a formal proposal for your final project, including an annotated bibliography of sources. More details will be provided here.
  • Final Project (25%): This will be a student-driven research project. More details will be provided here.
  • Class Participation (19%): In a small seminar such as this one, class participation is vital to the success of the course. Students are expected to not simply attend class, but to actively engage with the course materials and each other.
  • Class Mixtape (1%): Before the end of the semester, email me two songs that you would like to be included on the class mixtape. I'll assemble it for us and post a download on the website.
  • Note: This class does not have a final exam.

Grades are determined using a plus/minus scale: 97-100 A+, 93-96 A, 90-92 A-, etc. Generally speaking, major assignments that are completed on time and meet the basic requirements are worth a C. Work that demonstrates critical thinking, enthusiasm, and clarity will likely be in the A and B range, with As reserved for truly exceptional work.
